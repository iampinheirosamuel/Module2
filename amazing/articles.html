More Than the Cover

By Joy Dorcas

A journey with blue print clearly laid out and map clearly understood. A smooth 
harmless trip with no sharp bend nor hill climbing to the best of destination, life of 
roses, grasses flourishing with no thorn. So are the thought of an innocent child 
towards life… Alas! Something different taking place, the weather is actually a 
mixture of summer, spring, autumn and winter, the topography of the trip actually 
has bends, hill, valleys and some water bodies to be crossed, and there are actually 
thorns in the harmless looking vegetation’s that must be carefully watched. What 
a puncture on a lightly flowing balloon…
Awakening to reality arises, preparation for the trip ahead start, tones of 
practices, lots of studying, planning, prayers and adjustment taking place. The issue 
actually is not about the difference between what it seems to be and what it is 
actually but the approach to it. The challenges are to be conquered, quests to be 
completed, examinations to be passed and the battles to be won. 
Looking at it from another point of view, success would not be valuable without 
the existence of failure nor life if not for death nor wealth in the absence of poverty 
neither health if not for sickness. It would just be a boring trip because everything 
is known, equal and similar to all at the same time. 
Life is beautiful and valuable, ruled by principles you need to discover yourself, an 
individual race in the midst of many. It can be so simple or as complex as a web, 
you request what you want and stand at it. You are the determinant of what 
happens to you. Yes! God has placed in your hand a treasure you need His wisdom 
to manage. You being a determinant here is handling over the building of the house 
to the one who drew the plan and owns the construction company. The choice is 
yours but wisdom is following the one with the master plan and road map for the 
trip. Indeed life is more the cover, yet you can make it the best of it.

SHARE WITH YOUR FRIENDS! 

Friends

By Salimonu Abiola

A Chronicle of a Student

Hmm….my cat has nine lives they say…. They don’t know it has been only God who 

has been there for me even when things seem lost and hopeless. They feel am really 

boxed up or something, and the last time I checked I am still my father’s daughter 

not Otedola’s!!!!. To the fact that when I tell people am broke, they don’t believe 

me. 

Some expect others to clean their mess because they are related or what?... I 

wonder! Some can be annoying while others lack courtesy. Really, even as friends, 

courtesy demands being polite, not hurting your friend intentionally nor

inconveniencing your friend also. People fail to realize this and it can be so painful. 

Don’t remember that friend either when you are broke, have nothing to eat or need 

him or her to help fix something alone, which is too bad. You should also remember 

them when you have a full pot of soup or lots of money to spend. Do unto others 

what you want them to do to you! To that your friend whom you think has 

everything going on well for her, all might not be rosy for him/her dear. So, don’t 

be a burden by using your friends as mess fixers. Some friends are so loyal that they 

won’t complain, but they will disappoint rather than complain. Some will start 

avoiding you and your calls, they will refuse to share certain secrets and 

information with you. Watch out and be a better friend! Don’t add to your friend’s 

burden! Be a burden lifter! Help a friend today! To me, to you, it’s up to us to make 

the world a better place……

I Know Your Pain

By Pinheiro Samuel

Carting wheels of bicycle tires,

Off the street without direction,

Our whole life lies before us,

Like the palms beside the beach,

We go beyond our reach as we got older.

Fortified with strength but wasted,

As we seek to make ends “meats”,

Not because we didn’t love schooling,

But the opportunities we held on to let us down,

We kept falling off the shaking ladder.

Broken by disappointments and depressions,

Our souls crushed within for hopes are now on wheel chairs,

Every last attempt failed not because we are failures,

Even though we have been called that once, 

Yet we again push our hopes on wheelchairs.

We ask, is there a difference in our being?

No……….., Stop!

I remember the wheels of bicycles we cart off the street,

Yes! I remember! We did hung it aside, 

After every other day’s play, Yes!

We aren’t giving up yet,

We’ll hang the wheelchairs of our hopes, 

The day’s play are over,

Let hope arise!

Arise, Walk Hope!

Keys To Effective Living

By Pinheiro Samuel

“Imagine living everyday as though it was your last”   what would you have done 

differently today? Our everyday lives ought to be tales of memorable events which 

has in itself the satisfaction and fulfillment of starting a day and ending a day 

successfully. Teach us to number our days and recognize how few they are; help us 

to spend them as we should? This was written over 4,000 years ago by the Psalmist, 

the wisdom in those few lines of words are inexhaustible. The fulfillment of our 

purpose in life is the sum of our daily continual accomplishments in things that we 

have been born to do i.e. things that really counts in our lives. All of our life’s worth 

is a function of the values we have contributed to life on a daily basis; it’s not in the 

number of houses, car, expensive clothes and jewelries we possess.

Hence, we must learn to evaluate ourselves on a daily basis to realize how much 

we really worth. Examine your daily routine, what significant role do you play in life 

daily? Take thoughts of your actions, would you have welcome the same 

mannerism of your actions by other people? Look deep within, imagine today was 

your last on earth, what would you have do differently today?
